Median Household Income vs. Obesity

From the graph we are able to see that there is a negative correclation between the median household income per state and the percentage of the population that is obese.
The District of Columbia, which has the highest median pay at $46,000, has the lowest obesity percentage at 21%. There is one outlier in the dataset which is Peurto Rico.
Rico, though having the lowest median incomes at $16,669, has an obesity percentage that falls in the middle of the range at 28%.
